Since 2018

One roaster, a rented kitchen, and a stubborn idea

Kopi & Co. started small and stayed personal on purpose. Here's the short version of how we got here.

The timeline

How we got here

2018

  A kitchen, a hand grinder, and Saturday markets

Nadeesha started roasting small batches at home and selling bags at the Kalutara weekend market, alongside a day job. The first roaster held 1kg at a time.

2020

  The first counter

A four-table shopfront on Galle Road opened with one espresso machine, bought secondhand and rebuilt by hand. Most days we sold out by 2pm.

2022

  Direct relationships with growers

We started buying directly from two estates in Matale and Kandy, paying above the standard rate and visiting the farms each season.

2024

  The room you sit in today

We knocked through to the unit next door, added the window seating and the kitchen, and started serving breakfast all day.

Today

  Still the same crew

Six years on, three of our first five hires are still behind the counter. We're still roasting twice a week, just in slightly bigger batches.

What we hold onto

Three things that haven't changed

01

Pay growers properly

We buy direct, above standard market rate, and visit the estates we source from at least once a season.

02

Roast in small lots

Never more than 12kg at a time, twice weekly, so what's in your cup was roasted within the week.

03

Know your name

We're not trying to be everywhere. We'd rather have one room where the staff actually know the regulars.

Where it comes from

Two estates, one relationship each

Matale Estate, mid-country

Our house espresso blend base — chocolate, low acidity, grown at around 600m.

Kandy Hills Estate, high-grown

Used for our rotating pour-over — brighter, floral, harvested once a year.

Roasted on-site in Kalutara

Every batch is roasted at the shop, logged, and tasted before it goes on the shelf.
